# Policies

Policies are static global modifiers selected during the Seeding Phase.
Each player starts the game with two active policies. Policies are not
part of the seeding deck and do not count toward deck size or rarity
limits.

## Structure

A policy can have up to three components:

- **Passive effect** — always active during the main phase.
- **Seeding effect** — modifies the seeding phase in some way. Only
  applies if the policy is selected before or during seeding.
- **Policy action** — a special action the player can take during the
  main phase, costing AP and/or gold as printed.

## Selection

See Seeding Phase in the master rules for the full selection process.
The first policy is chosen before draft rounds, the second after deck
construction. A policy's seeding effect only applies if the policy is
active during seeding (i.e. only the first selected policy can have a
seeding effect).

## Examples

**Scholar**
- *Seeding*: After deck construction, look at the top 5 cards of your
  main deck and reorder them.
- *Passive*: Draw +1 card at start of turn.
- *Action — Investigate (1 AP)*: Look at one face-down dilemma at any
  mission location.

**Warlord**
- *Passive*: Gain 1 gold each time one of your units wins combat. Raze
  costs 2 AP instead of 3.
- *Action — Rally (1 AP)*: Your killed Warrior units go to the top of
  your main deck instead of your discard pile until end of turn.
